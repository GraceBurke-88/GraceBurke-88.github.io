IP Addresses, Packets, and Routing

IP Addresses

Internet Protocol Address
Numerical label that is given to each device
Example: 123.123.123.123
Allows one computer to communicate with another computer.
Could be compared to a house address, since are used as identification and finding locations.

Packets

A small amount of data that is sent over a network, such as the internet.
In the mail analogy the ‘packet’ can be thought of as a letter containing data, it has a source (home address) and a destination.
Packets are broken up pieces of a larger amount of data, this means that if one piece is dropped, that is the only thing that has to be sent again.

Routing

Routing is how packets are moved from their initial source to another destination
There are many paths packets can take to reach their final destination
Packets are sent and reassembled when the reach their final destination
Routers are like ‘traffic managers’, they keep the packets moving smoothly
Packets may arrive at different times or out of order
Routers will select the ‘cheapest’ path, this means the path that is the fastest and makes the most sense politically
Having several options makes networks ‘fault tolerant,’ which means data can still be sent, it if there is a major issue with one of the routes

TCP

Transmission Control Protocol
Ensures that all the packets are delivered.
If something is missing, TCP will request it to be resent
